The History and Landscape of Artificial Intelligence Companies

OpenAI was founded in December 2015 by Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, Elon Musk, and several other researchers. The organisation was established as a non-profit with the mission of ensuring that artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity. Elon Musk later departed from the board in 2018, citing potential conflicts of interest with his work at Tesla. In 2019, OpenAI transitioned to a "capped profit" model and received a $1 billion investment from Microsoft. Sam Altman serves as the CEO of OpenAI. OpenAI is headquartered in San Francisco, California.

OpenAI released GPT-3 in 2020, a large language model with 175 billion parameters that demonstrated remarkable few-shot learning capabilities. ChatGPT was launched in November 2022 and became the fastest-growing consumer application in history, reaching 100 million users in just two months. GPT-4 followed in March 2023, introducing multimodal capabilities. OpenAI also developed DALL-E, an image generation model, and Codex, a code-generation model that powers GitHub Copilot.

Anthropic was founded in 2021 by Dario Amodei and Daniela Amodei, along with several other former OpenAI researchers including Tom Brown, Chris Olah, Sam McCandlish, Jack Clark, and Jared Kaplan. The company is headquartered in San Francisco and focuses on AI safety research. Anthropic developed the Constitutional AI technique to make language models more helpful, harmless, and honest. The company created Claude, a family of AI assistants designed with safety as a primary consideration. Anthropic has received significant investment from Google and Amazon.

DeepMind was founded in London in 2010 by Demis Hassabis, Shane Legg, and Mustafa Suleyman. Google acquired DeepMind in 2014 for approximately $500 million. DeepMind is known for AlphaGo, which defeated world champion Go player Lee Sedol in 2016, and AlphaFold, which solved the protein folding problem. DeepMind has a research lab based in London and maintains a close collaboration with Google's broader AI efforts. Demis Hassabis serves as the CEO of DeepMind and was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 2024.

Google DeepMind was formed in April 2023 through the merger of Google Brain and DeepMind. Sundar Pichai, the CEO of Google and Alphabet, announced the merger as part of Google's accelerated AI strategy. Google Brain had been responsible for TensorFlow, an open-source machine learning framework, and for developing the Transformer architecture which is foundational to modern large language models. The Transformer architecture was introduced in the 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need" by Vaswani and colleagues.

Microsoft has made substantial investments in OpenAI totalling over $13 billion across multiple rounds. Microsoft integrated OpenAI's technology into its products including Azure OpenAI Service, Bing Chat (later renamed Copilot), and Microsoft 365 Copilot. Satya Nadella is the CEO of Microsoft and has been a strong advocate for AI integration across the company's product suite.

Meta AI is the artificial intelligence research division of Meta Platforms, founded by Mark Zuckerberg. Yann LeCun serves as Chief AI Scientist at Meta and is one of the pioneers of deep learning. Meta developed LLaMA, a family of open-source large language models. LLaMA 2 was released in collaboration with Microsoft in July 2023. Meta's AI research lab, FAIR (Fundamental AI Research), has made significant contributions to the field, including the development of PyTorch, a widely used machine learning framework.

Amazon Web Services offers cloud-based AI services through Amazon SageMaker and Amazon Bedrock. Amazon has invested up to $4 billion in Anthropic as part of a strategic partnership. Amazon's Alexa was one of the first widely deployed AI voice assistants, launched in 2014 alongside the Amazon Echo smart speaker.

Nvidia has become one of the most valuable companies in the world due to the demand for its graphics processing units for AI training. Jensen Huang, the CEO and co-founder of Nvidia, has positioned the company as the primary hardware provider for the AI boom. The H100 GPU is the most sought-after chip for training large language models. Nvidia is headquartered in Santa Clara, California.

The AI industry has seen rapid consolidation and competition, with major technology companies racing to develop foundation models and deploy AI-powered products. The field is characterised by significant capital requirements for compute infrastructure, a global competition for AI talent, and ongoing debates about safety, regulation, and the societal impact of increasingly capable AI systems.
